Watering 101

The #1 killer of houseplants is overwatering. Learn the finger test: push your finger 2 inches into the soil. If dry, water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom. If moist, wait a few days.

  • check_circleWater less in winter
  • check_circleUse room-temperature water
  • check_circleEnsure pots have drainage holes
light mode

Light Requirements

Understanding light is key. "Bright indirect" means near a window but not in direct sun rays. Most tropical plants burn in direct afternoon sun but thrive in morning light.

  • check_circleNorth-facing = low light
  • check_circleEast-facing = bright indirect
  • check_circleRotate plants quarterly

Humidity & Temperature

Tropical plants crave humidity above 50%. Group plants together, use pebble trays, or invest in a humidifier. Keep away from heaters and AC vents.

  • check_circleMist leaves weekly
  • check_circleKeep 65-80°F (18-27°C)
  • check_circleAvoid cold drafts
compost

Soil & Repotting

Most houseplants prefer well-draining potting mix. Repot every 1-2 years or when roots circle the bottom. Go only 1-2 inches larger in pot size.

bug_report

Pest Prevention

Inspect new plants before bringing them home. Wipe leaves monthly with neem oil solution. Isolate any infested plants immediately to prevent spreading.

nutrition

Fertilizing

Feed during growing season (spring-summer) with diluted liquid fertilizer every 2-4 weeks. Reduce or stop in fall and winter when growth slows.
